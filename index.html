HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels

The data type in this visualization is qualitative/text. In the mind map, the marks are points and lines. The lines connect the points together to make the map design. The points are used as an item/node and the lines are used as links. The channels are color, horizontal and vertical position, and length of the lines. There are different colors based on how far away the each point is from the main point.

This visualization is the combination of a a pie chart and a bubble chart. A bubble chart is similar to a scatterplot, however it uses size to show a third variable. The data type is quantitative. In this visualization, the marks are areas, more specifically circles. The marks are used as items/nodes. The channels are horizontal and vertical position, color, and size (area). The x-axis goes from 0 to 10 and the y-axis goes from 0 to 1000.

The bump chart is comprised of quantitative data. There are four years at the top: 2005, 2015, 2015, 2015. There are rows titled "A", "B", "C", "D", and "E". The marks are both points and lines. The lines connect different points together. The points are used as an item/node and the lines are used as links. The channels of this visualization are position, both horizontal and vertical, and color. It is unclear what each color represents in this visualization.

The stacked bar graph involves more data than a standard bar graph. The stacked bar graph displays a numerical value based on two different categorical value. So, there are two datatypes in this visualization: quantitative and categorical. As there is no legend on this visualization, the levels of one of the categorical variables is unknown, but the other categorical variable has levels of "A", "B", "C", and "D".
